Privacy Policy

LabTracker is a personal health data tool that lets you track blood test results, protocols, and biomarker trends over time. Your privacy — especially concerning your health data — is fundamental to how we build this product.

The short version: LabTracker works entirely without an account. If you choose to create one, your health data is stored in the EU, encrypted in transit, and never sold or shared. You can export or delete everything at any time.

1. Who We Are

LabTracker is operated by LabTracker ("we", "us", "our"). We are the data controller for any personal data processed through the LabTracker web application at labtracker.app.

For any privacy-related questions or requests, you can reach us at: trtlabtracker@gmail.com

2. How LabTracker Works: Two Modes

Local Mode default

By default, LabTracker operates entirely in your browser. All data — blood test results, biomarker values, protocols, and parsed PDF content — is stored locally on your device using browser storage. No data is transmitted to our servers. We cannot access, read, or recover your local data. No account is required.

Cloud Mode opt-in

If you choose to create an account, your data is synchronized to our cloud infrastructure so you can access it across devices and benefit from automatic backups. Cloud mode requires explicit consent for health data processing at the time of registration. You can switch back to local-only mode at any time.

We designed LabTracker with a local-first architecture specifically to give you a fully private option. Creating an account is never required.

4. Health Data & Special Protections

Blood test results, biomarker values, and protocol information (such as medications, hormone treatments, and supplement regimens) constitute health data — a special category of personal data under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, Article 9) and similar laws worldwide.

We process health data only when you have given us explicit consent to do so. This consent is collected separately from general terms acceptance when you create a cloud account. You can withdraw this consent at any time by switching to local mode or deleting your account.

We apply the following additional protections to health data:

  • Health data is never used for advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than providing LabTracker's core functionality to you.
  • Health data is never sold, rented, or shared with third parties for their own purposes.
  • Health data is isolated per user through database-level access controls (Row Level Security), meaning it is technically impossible for one user to access another user's data through the application.
  • Health data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) between your browser and our servers.
  • We do not log, cache, or store health data in any analytics, error tracking, or monitoring systems.

7. Where Your Data Is Stored

Cloud mode data is stored in Supabase's EU region (Ireland, eu-west-1), hosted on Amazon Web Services infrastructure within the European Economic Area. This ensures your health data remains subject to EU data protection standards.

When you use Google login, authentication tokens are exchanged with Google's servers (US-based). However, your health data is never transmitted to Google. The authentication process only verifies your identity.

For users accessing LabTracker from outside the EU, please note that you are sending data to EU-based servers. The EU provides strong data protection standards that benefit all users regardless of location.

8. How Long We Keep Your Data

  • Active account: Your data is retained for as long as your account is active.
  • Account deletion: When you delete your account, all associated data (profile, blood test results, biomarkers, protocols) is permanently deleted from our database within 30 days. Backups that may contain your data are purged within 90 days.
  • Local mode data: Data stored in your browser persists until you clear your browser data or use LabTracker's built-in data management tools. We have no access to or control over locally stored data.
  • Authentication logs: Basic authentication event logs (login timestamps, not health data) may be retained for up to 90 days for security purposes.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. We honor these rights for all users, regardless of jurisdiction:

  • Access: You can view all data associated with your account directly within LabTracker at any time.
  • Portability: You can export all your data in a machine-readable JSON format using the built-in export function.
  • Rectification: You can edit or correct your data at any time within the application.
  • Erasure: You can delete your entire account and all associated data. This action is permanent and cannot be undone.
  • Restrict processing: You can switch to local mode at any time, which stops all server-side data processing.
  • Withdraw consent: You can withdraw your consent for health data processing at any time by switching to local mode or deleting your account. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Lodge a complaint: If you are in the EU/EEA, you have the right to file a complaint with your local data protection authority. A list of EU DPAs is available at edpb.europa.eu.

To exercise any of these rights, you can use the built-in tools in LabTracker or contact us at trtlabtracker@gmail.com. We will respond to all requests within 30 days.

12. Security Measures

We implement the following technical and organizational measures to protect your data:

  • All data in transit is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Database access is controlled through Row Level Security (RLS) policies, enforced at the database level, ensuring complete data isolation between users.
  • Authentication passwords are hashed using bcrypt and are never stored in plaintext.
  • API keys and secrets are stored as environment variables and are never exposed in client-side code.
  • The application is served exclusively over HTTPS.
  • Authentication tokens are stored in secure, HttpOnly cookies to prevent cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks.
  • We conduct periodic reviews of our security practices and dependencies.

While we take extensive measures to protect your data, no system is 100% secure. If you discover a security vulnerability, please report it responsibly to trtlabtracker@gmail.com.

13. Additional Rights for California Residents

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to know: You can request details about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.
  • Right to delete: You can request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to opt-out of sale/sharing: We do not sell or share your personal information as defined by the CCPA/CPRA. There is nothing to opt out of.
  • Right to non-discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your privacy rights.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information: Health data is classified as sensitive personal information under the CCPA. We use it solely to provide you with LabTracker's core functionality.

To exercise these rights, contact us at trtlabtracker@gmail.com. We will verify your identity before processing any request.

14. International Users

LabTracker is accessible worldwide. Regardless of where you are located, we apply the same high standard of data protection to all users. Specifically:

  • European Economic Area (EEA), UK, Switzerland: Your data is stored within the EU (Ireland). We comply with the GDPR and UK GDPR. Our legal basis for processing health data is your explicit consent.
  • United States: We comply with applicable state privacy laws including the CCPA/CPRA (California), Washington My Health My Data Act, and similar state-level legislation.
  • Canada: We process data in accordance with PIPEDA principles, including consent, limited collection, and individual access.
  • Brazil: We comply with the LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados), which provides rights similar to the GDPR.
  • All other regions: We apply GDPR-equivalent protections to all users as our baseline standard.
